I RESEARCH
THE CHANGING COSMOS

KILONOVAE

Kilonovae are the optical transients that are associated with the merger of two neutron stars, or the merger of a neutron star with a black hole. The mergers emits strong gravitational radiation at coalescence and provide the Universe with the all the heaviest elements, including gold, silver, and platinum. I primarily study the formation of the elements from an observational perspective.

SUPERNOVAE

Supernovae marks the death of most massive stars. They are powered by the ash products of their stellar parent and provide the majority of the material that makes up the rocky planets, like Earth - including the oxygen that keeps you alive. They come in many different shapes and colors and I try to map out these characteristics, along with what consequences they have for their host galaxies.

GAMMA-RAY BURSTS

Gamma-ray bursts are the intense flashes of radiation that are emitted when then heaviest stars collapse to black holes. The light from a gamma-ray burst is so bright that it momentarily rivals that of all the stars in the universe, combined. This extreme brightness makes them visible all the way to the edge of the observable universe and therefore equisite probes of the cosmochemic assebly history of the universe.

QUASARS

Quasars are immensely bright objects, powered by the continous mass accretion onto supermassive black holes. The energy output of the quasars regulates the stellar mass growth of their host galaxies and powers part of the cosmic epoch of re-ionization. The brightness, similar to gamma-ray bursts, makes this astronomical phenomena extremely useful for probing the intervening universe.
